The best problem-solving books for electrodynamics include David J. Griffiths’ Introduction to Electrodynamics for foundational, intuitive problems, and Purcell and Morin’s Electricity and Magnetism for challenging, physical-insight problems. For advanced numerical practice, Jackson’s Classical Electrodynamics is the standard, while Zangwill’s Modern Electrodynamics bridges the gap between undergraduate and graduate levels.Here are top recommended problem-solving electrodynamics books:Best Overall for Undergraduates: Introduction to Electrodynamics by David J. Griffiths – Known for excellent, clear, and challenging problem sets that build physical intuition.Best for Deep Physical Intuition: Electricity and Magnetism by Edward M. Purcell and David J. Morin – Highly recommended for its challenging, "fun," and imaginative exercises.Best for Graduate-Level/Rigorous Problems: Classical Electrodynamics by John David Jackson – Known as the "standard" graduate text, featuring notoriously difficult and comprehensive problems.Best Modern Alternative to Jackson: Modern Electrodynamics by Andrew Zangwill – Highly regarded for being more readable than Jackson while covering advanced, modern topics.Best for Solved/Exam Problems:Schaum's Outline of Electromagnetics – Excellent for step-by-step solutions to numerical problems.Problems in Classical Electrodynamics by Brau – Focuses on modern, practical applications.I.E.
